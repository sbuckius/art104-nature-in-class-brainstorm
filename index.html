A Note on AI (Automation)

Sarah used AI (automation) to help construct the html to setup this tool by prompting it with details about the idea, the features, the parts, and the framework. Sarah believes that many things should not be automated using AI and that users should be cautious about the inaccuracies and bias in the system, exploitation of workers, the environmental impact, and the unethical use of the work of people without compensation, consent, or attribution.

While Sarah still struggles with the issues related to how AI (automation) could impact humans and the world, Sarah is experimenting with using it to support education and equity in instances where it does not automate the work of humans that involves learning, creativity, and emotions. In this case, Sarah believes that this tool might help students to streamline the collaboration process and free them up for the fun, creative collaborative learning. However, the actual reality is unknown and students please share whether this helps or not.

And, the possibility of people becoming reliant on the technology is a concern, especially because the design, deployment, and use is in the power of a few individuals (without guardrails or oversight). Sarah worries about this reliance on the technology when those in power make the cost to use it high and this creates an equity issue.
